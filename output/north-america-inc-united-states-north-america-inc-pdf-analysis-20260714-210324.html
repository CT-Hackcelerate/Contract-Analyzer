    How to use this page

    Every service-level commitment we extract is scored automatically, in code — never by the AI — against a fixed benchmark playbook, so the verdict is deterministic and cannot be invented. Read it in three steps:

    1. Scorecard — how many SLAs meet the standard, fall below it, or carry an uncapped penalty.
    2. This contract vs standard — each SLA's extracted target beside the applicable standard and the verdict:
      ✓ Meets standard the stated target meets or beats the benchmark (pass).   ⚠ Below standard the stated target is worse than the benchmark (fail — negotiate).   ⚠ Uncapped penalty a credit/penalty exists with no cap (fail — negotiate).   No target stated the SLA clause was found but no measurable value was given (missing — verify manually).
    3. Reference standards — the full playbook the verdicts are based on.

    What is and isn't auto-scored. Only numeric threshold checks get a pass/fail verdict above (a percentage or time the tool can read and compare). Everything else in the Reference Standards below — Presence ("is this protection in the contract at all?"), Quality, Consistency, Risk-flag and Evidence checks, including the Supplier and AI/Token safeguards and the ARR/funding items — is shown as the house standard for human review and is NOT auto-graded. A blank or absence there does not mean the contract complies; a reviewer must confirm it. Source: internal SLA / SOW / Project-Risk playbook (A–W).
